When "Perfect" Still Fails: A Lesson in Advocacy

By Dylan Worth
September 2, 2025 · 4 min read

In this field we live by the details. Sometimes the job looks perfect. Then the lab says otherwise. This story is about the moment I should have pushed for full removal instead of sanding.

The crossroads

Two water losses. Several floor layers. The question was simple. Remove the stubborn layer or sand and clean. My gut said remove. I stayed quiet. The consensus leaned toward sanding.

The result

The space looked flawless. The IAQ failed. Not due to poor cleaning. The failure came from a decision I did not argue against.

The lesson

My job is not only to scrub and seal. It is to be a voice for the science in the room. When removal is the right call, no amount of sanding will replace it. I will speak up next time. That protects the client and the standard of care.

Perfection is not enough. Advocacy is.

Zombie Spider in a Crawl Space

By Dylan Worth
September 1, 2025 · Field Notes

The white tendrils and bulbs on the spider are the fruiting bodies of a fungus. These fungi target arthropods. The spider is not alive. The fungus is using the carcass as a base to spread spores. It is a sign of humidity and poor ventilation in the crawl space.

Walk‑In Cooler Biofilm

By Dylan Worth
August 30, 2025 · Field Notes

Cold damp surfaces with food residue support biofilm. Gram negative bacteria can produce a slime layer that resists light cleaning. Disrupt the film with scrubbing. Use an EPA registered disinfectant that is safe for food areas. Clean drains and keep a routine.
