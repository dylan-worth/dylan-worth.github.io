The House of Habsburg

"A.E.I.O.U." (All the world is subject to Austria)

The House of Habsburg was one of the most influential and distinguished royal houses of Europe. The throne of the Holy Roman Empire was continuously occupied by the Habsburgs from 1438 until their extinction in the male line in 1740.

Empire of the Sun: Under Charles V, the Habsburgs controlled a vast territory that spanned Europe and the Americas, leading to the phrase: "The empire on which the sun never sets."

Marriage Diplomacy

The Habsburgs were famous for expanding their power through matrimonial alliances rather than war. Their motto regarding this was: Bella gerant alii, tu felix Austria nube ("Let others wage war; you, happy Austria, marry").

The Double-Headed Eagle

As Holy Roman Emperors, they adopted the double-headed eagle as their heraldic symbol. One head looked toward the East (Rome/Byzantium) and the other toward the West, signifying their claim to universal imperial authority over the Christian world.

The family's reign finally came to an end in 1918 following World War I, marking the dissolution of the Austro-Hungarian Empire.
