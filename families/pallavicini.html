The House of Pallavicini

The Pallavicini family is one of the most ancient noble lineages in Italy. Descending from the Obertenghi line, they established themselves as "Stato Pallavicino"—a small but sovereign state between Parma and Piacenza that they ruled for centuries.

The Double Allegiance: The family split into several branches, notably in Genoa and Austria. They were so influential that they held high positions in the British, Austrian, and Italian courts simultaneously.

The Genoese Bankers

In the 16th and 17th centuries, the Genoese branch became world-class financiers, providing the capital that fueled the Spanish Empire's global expansion.
