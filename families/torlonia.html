The House of Torlonia

Unlike the ancient Roman houses, the Torlonia family rose to prominence through the textile trade and banking for the Vatican. They are often cited as the wealthiest family in Rome during the 19th century.

Draining the Lake: Alessandro Torlonia is famous for successfully draining the Fucino Lake—a feat the Roman Emperors failed to do—earning him the title of Prince and vast agricultural lands.

The Marbles

The Torlonia Marbles represent the most significant private collection of ancient sculptures in the world, rivaling even the Vatican's own collections.
