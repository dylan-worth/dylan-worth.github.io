The House of Colonna

"Moles In Ampla Columna" (A column in a great mass)

The House of Colonna is a pillar of the Roman aristocracy. For centuries, they were the "Princes Assistants to the Pontifical Throne," a hereditary honor that cemented their status at the very top of the Papal hierarchy.

The Great Naval Hero: The family’s most celebrated military achievement came in 1571. Marcantonio Colonna served as the Admiral of the Papal fleet during the Battle of Lepanto, where the Holy League decisively defeated the Ottoman Empire, changing the course of European history.

The Orsini Rivalry

Medieval Rome was often a battlefield between the Colonna (Ghibellines) and the Orsini (Guelfs). This centuries-long feud was so intense that it was eventually resolved only by a Papal Bull in 1511, followed by a marriage between the two families to end the bloodshed.

Palazzo Colonna

Their family seat, Palazzo Colonna, is one of the oldest and largest private palaces in Rome. Much of it was built over the remains of an ancient Roman temple. The Great Hall (Galleria Colonna) is a stunning display of Baroque opulence, designed to celebrate the family's victory at Lepanto.

Unlike many noble houses, the Colonna family still owns and resides in their ancestral palace today, maintaining one of the world's most significant private art collections.

The Symbolism

Their coat of arms is elegantly simple: a single silver column with a golden base and capital, topped with a golden crown (added by Emperor Ludwig IV). It symbolizes their name and their self-perception as the immovable support of the Roman state.
