The House of Saxe-Gotha

The Duchy of Saxe-Gotha was one of the Saxon duchies held by the Ernestine branch of the Wettin dynasty. It represents the pinnacle of German dynastic politics during the 17th and 18th centuries.

The Progenitor: This line is critical to modern royalty; it eventually merged into Saxe-Coburg and Gotha, the very house that would provide kings to Belgium, Portugal, Bulgaria, and the United Kingdom.

The Gotha Almanac

The name Gotha became synonymous with European nobility thanks to the "Almanach de Gotha," the definitive directory of Europe's royalty and higher nobility, first published in their capital city.
