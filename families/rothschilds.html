The 1917 Pivot: On November 2, 1917, Foreign Secretary Arthur James Balfour writes an important letter to Britain's most illustrious Jewish citizen, Baron Lionel Walter Rothschild, expressing the British government's support for a Jewish homeland in Palestine. The letter would eventually become known as the Balfour Declaration.

The Five Arrows

Mayer Amschel sent his five sons to London, Paris, Frankfurt, Vienna, and Naples. This ensured that no matter which European power was at war, a Rothschild was on the winning side of the treasury.

The "Red Shield"

The name originates from the Haus zum Roten Schild. The family transitioned from coin dealers to the "Bankers to Kings," famously funding the British effort in the Napoleonic Wars.

Land & Infrastructure

Beyond banking, the family was instrumental in the industrial revolution. They funded the first railways in France and Austria, the mining of Rio Tinto, and the acquisition of the Suez Canal for the British Crown.

Bio information...

CIRCA 1567 Isaak Elchanan Bacharach

The first to adopt the name. He built the "Haus zum Roten Schild" (House of the Red Shield) in Frankfurt's Judengasse.

1710 — 1755 Amschel Moses Rothschild

A money changer and silk trader. Father of the dynasty's true architect.

1744 — 1812 Mayer Amschel Rothschild

The Founding Father. He established the banking house and was named "Crown Agent." He formulated the plan to send his sons across Europe.

1773 — 1855 The Five Arrows (The Sons)

Amschel, Salomon, Nathan, Calmann, and Jakob. They expanded the name into a global banking network.

LATE 19TH - 20TH CENTURY The Victorian & Edwardian Eras

The family funds the Suez Canal (1875) and transitions into massive philanthropic and political roles across Europe.

1936 — 2024 Jacob Rothschild, 4th Baron Rothschild

A major figure in the London branch and Chairman of RIT Capital Partners. He was a massive patron of the arts and heritage in the UK.

ACTIVE 2026 The Seventh Generation

The family continues to operate through Rothschild & Co and Edmond de Rothschild Group, maintaining a global presence in private banking and asset management.
