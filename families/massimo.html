The House of Massimo

"Cunctando Restituit" (By Delaying, He Restored)

The House of Massimo is one of the oldest and most distinguished aristocratic families of Rome. As members of the "Black Nobility"—the Roman families that remained loyal to the Papacy after the fall of the Papal States in 1870—they hold a unique position in Italian history.

The Ancient Claim: The family is legendary for its claim of descent from the Gens Fabia of Ancient Rome. When Napoleon Bonaparte famously asked Prince Massimo if it were true that his family descended from Fabius Maximus, the Prince replied: "I cannot actually prove it, but it is a rumor that has run in my family for twelve hundred years."

Papal Service

Elevated to princely rank in the 18th century, the Massimo family has provided the Church with numerous cardinals, diplomats, and high-ranking officials. They were instrumental in the diplomatic maneuvers of the Renaissance and the Counter-Reformation.

Palazzo Massimo alle Colonne

The family's seat in Rome, the Palazzo Massimo alle Colonne, is a masterpiece of Mannerist architecture. Built on the site where the family has lived for centuries, it is famous for its curved facade that follows the lines of the ancient Theater of Domitian.

Every year on March 16th, the palace opens to the public to commemorate a miracle performed by Saint Philip Neri in 1583 involving a young member of the Massimo family.

The Motto

Their motto, Cunctando Restituit, refers to the "Delayer" (Fabius Maximus Cunctator), the Roman general who defeated Hannibal by wearing him down through attrition—a testament to the family's survival through patience and endurance.
