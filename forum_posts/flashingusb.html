Flashing the Drive

Dylan May 12, 2024
Step 1: Gather Your Tools

First up, snag yourself the latest Arch Linux ISO from their official website. Think of it as picking the right ingredients for your tech recipe. Next, get a USB stick with at least 2GB of free space. Make sure there’s nothing important on it because it’s about to get completely wiped.
Step 2: Choose Your Tool

Now, choose your weapon—I mean, tool. We’ve got three contenders:

Rufus (Windows): A fast and reliable choice that doesn’t mess around.
Etcher (Windows, Mac, Linux): The friendly, easy-to-use tool that's pretty much foolproof.
RosaImageWriter (Linux): A straightforward, no-frills option for Linux loyalists.

Step 3: Prepare for the Flash

Before you start, backup any precious data from your USB stick. It's going to get wiped cleaner than a slate on the first day of school.
Step 4: Flash Away!

With Rufus (Windows):

Open Rufus and insert your USB stick.
Select your USB drive in Rufus, pick the Arch ISO file, and simply click “Start.” Confirm that you want to erase the USB stick and then let Rufus do its magic.

With Etcher (Windows, Mac, Linux):

Launch Etcher and click “Flash from file” to select your Arch ISO.
Insert your USB stick, select it in Etcher, and hit “Flash!” Sit back and watch the progress bar.

With Rosa Image Writer (Linux):

Open Rosa Image Writer, choose your ISO file and the target USB drive.
Hit the “Write” button and give it a minute or two to complete the flash.

Step 5: Eject and Test

Once the flashing process is complete, safely eject your USB stick from your computer. You’re now ready to reboot and test out your new Arch Linux installer. Don’t forget to adjust your boot order in BIOS if needed.
Final Step: Begin Your Arch Installation

Pop that USB into the computer where you want to install Arch Linux, boot it up, and follow the Arch installation guide to set up your new system. It's like following a recipe, just stick to the steps!

And that’s it—no magic, just a straightforward tech task with the right tools. Enjoy setting up your sleek, new Arch Linux system!
